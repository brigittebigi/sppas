 52  """ 
 53  James Allen, in 1983, proposed an algebraic framework named Interval Algebra 
 54  (IA), for qualitative reasoning with time intervals where the binary 
 55  relationship between a pair of intervals is represented  by a subset of 13 
 56  atomic relation. 
 57   
 58  In 1983 James F. Allen published a paper in which he proposed 13 basic relations 
 59  between time intervals that are distinct, exhaustive, and qualitative: 
 60    - distinct because no pair of definite intervals can be related 
 61    by more than one of the relationships; 
 62    - exhaustive because any pair of definite intervals are described 
 63    by one of the relations; 
 64    - qualitative (rather than quantitative) because no numeric time 
 65    spans are considered. 
 66   
 67  These relations and the operations on them form Allen's Interval Algebra. 
 68  Using this calculus, given facts can be formalized and then used for 
 69  automatic reasoning. 
 70  Temporal representation and reasoning is an important facet in the design 
 71  of many intelligent systems. For example, question answering systems require 
 72  at least some basic temporal representation scheme to answer temporal 
 73  questions. 
 74   
 75  """
